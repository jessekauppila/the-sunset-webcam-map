# LACMA Art + Technology Lab Grant — Project Context

## The Project: *Eternal Golden Hour*
*(working title — rename as needed)*

A continuously live-streamed global feed of sunrises and sunsets, running 24/7, algorithmically curated from a distributed network of cameras placed at terminator zones, the liminal boundary where day turns to night and night to day around the planet.

### Core Technical Components
- **Terminator zone mapping** using deck.gl to identify real-time day/night boundaries globally
- **Webcam discovery + aggregation** from existing public sources near active terminator zones
- **ML beauty algorithm** trained on labeled sunset/sunrise imagery to rank and select the most visually compelling feeds in real time
- **Distributed Raspberry Pi network** — low-cost custom camera nodes placed by a community of participants (Kickstarter model), expanding geographic coverage over time
- **Database of curated light events** — a growing archive of beautiful moments of planetary transition

### Artistic Framing
The project asks: *Can an algorithm find beauty? And if so, whose beauty does it learn?*

The terminator zone is one of the most ancient human symbols — the threshold between darkness and light — reframed here as a data problem and a collective act of attention. The live stream is not just a feed; it is a portrait of the planet exhaling and inhaling, perpetually, as seen through thousands of human-placed eyes.

The distributed camera network democratizes participation: anyone, anywhere, can become a node in a global artwork. The Raspberry Pi units are not just hardware — they are invitations to observe, to contribute, and to be part of something planetary in scale.

## Key Talking Points for the Proposal Narrative

1. **Lead with the question, not the system.** What does collective human attention to light tell us about our relationship to the planet? What gets encoded when a machine learns to find a moment beautiful?

2. **The terminator zone as concept.** The boundary between day and night is one of the oldest human metaphors. Reframing it as a data layer — something to be watched, parsed, and streamed — is a genuinely contemporary artistic gesture.

3. **The ML beauty problem is philosophically rich.** Whose aesthetic gets encoded in the training data? What cultural biases shape what the algorithm finds beautiful? Addressing this critically in the proposal shows exactly the kind of self-awareness LACMA's program values.

4. **The distributed network is the social sculpture.** The Raspberry Pi community isn't just infrastructure — it's an invitation to collective observation. Frame it as building a community of watchers, not selling hardware.

5. **The gap narrative.** Don't hide the ten years — own it. It is the most interesting thing about this candidacy. The proposal should explain, briefly and confidently, that the artist went deep into the technologies this project requires, and is now bringing that expertise back to art.
